About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Southern California (USC). My research interests lie at the intersection of network optimization, traffic engineering in time-varying networks, and machine learning for systems. I also work on federated learning and large language model steering / mixture-of-agents.

Research

  • Network interdiction on trees – dynamic programming and optimization methods for edge and node removal to interdict facility reachability.
  • Traffic engineering for satellite / WAN networks – predictive optimization and learning-based TE over time-varying topologies.
  • Federated learning & carbon-aware training – methods such as FedGreen / FedGreenCS and WeiAvgCS for efficient, diverse, and greener FL.
  • LLM steering & mixture-of-agents – activation steering, MoE/MoA-style architectures, and evaluation of their capabilities.

Selected Projects

  • Interdicting Facilities in Tree Networks – dynamic programming algorithm with experiments against FR and Gurobi on large random trees.
  • Satellite Traffic Engineering – simulation of LEO-like constellations and TE formulations that couple prediction with routing decisions.
